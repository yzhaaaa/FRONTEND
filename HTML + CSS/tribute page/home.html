TWICE

TWICE is a South Korean girl group formed by JYP Entertainment through the reality survival program Sixteen in 2015. The group is composed of nine members—Nayeon, Jeongyeon, Momo, Sana, Jihyo, Mina, Dahyun, Chaeyoung, and Tzuyu—each bringing unique charm and talent that contribute to the group’s distinct identity. TWICE officially debuted on October 20, 2015, with the mini-album The Story Begins and its title track Like OOH-AHH, which became an instant hit and established them as one of the most promising new acts in K-pop.

Their rise to fame was marked by their 2016 single Cheer Up, which topped music charts and earned them the “Song of the Year” award at several major Korean award shows. Following this success, TWICE continued to dominate the industry with a string of hit songs such as TT, Likey, Heart Shaker, and What Is Love?. Their music, characterized by catchy melodies, bright visuals, and relatable lyrics, helped define the cheerful and energetic concept that became their trademark.

In addition to their success in South Korea, TWICE achieved significant popularity in Japan. They released Japanese versions of their hit songs and original Japanese albums, quickly becoming one of the best-selling K-pop girl groups in the country. Over time, they also began reaching international audiences, especially with their first English single, The Feels, in 2021, which entered Billboard’s global charts and introduced TWICE to a wider global fanbase.

Throughout their career, TWICE has been praised for their professionalism, strong group chemistry, and ability to adapt to changing music trends while maintaining their signature charm. They have successfully evolved from a bright, youthful image to a more mature and confident style, showing their growth as artists.

Today, TWICE stands as one of the most influential and successful girl groups in K-pop history, with millions of fans worldwide known as “ONCE.” Their continued dedication, teamwork, and passion for music have solidified their legacy as true icons of modern pop culture.
